I have a fascination with precise fictional locations. As a girl I loved reading Down the Rabbit Hole by Peter Abrahams and knowing this fictional detective, Ingrid, ‘lived’ down the road from me, in my home state, in a place I could imagine, almost touch.

I grew up. She didn’t. Echo Falls remains, a partial pastiche of real life Beacon Falls, and a partial world unto itself. I still dream of going there, and up north, to the real cold of Yellowknife, which is somehow both real and not in my mind, because it was part of that other world. Almost as far away as Narnia.

When I was a teenager I gulped down the first 6 seasons of Supernatural. The old ones, with the dim lighting and bloody openings. It made me laugh and cry and grip onto the particulars of this fictional world flitting around next to my own. This imperfect, silly, dead serious cult show will forever be in my heart and brain, and I find it peculiarly fitting that I have now applied my new, practical, real life mapping skills to show a few places that don’t exist.

Except they do, in the imagination, on the small screen, and in the dusty, never-really gone corners of the Internet. This marvelous list of Fictional Locations on the Supernatural Wiki, from which I created a little reminder of the power and joy of imagining possible worlds, precise and made up lat/long and all.
